What am I looking at?

The graphics below were all made for UCLA Circle K, our campus chapter of Circle K International, the largest collegiate service organization in the world. Most of them are social media graphics made to advertise club events. I currently serve as 2017-2018 Graphic Design Chair and as such lead the club's Graphic Design Committee. In each design I create, I try to create artwork that I can be proud of, that serves not only to inform but also to please. Oh, and I'm an Illustrator main. Enjoy!

Winter Leadership Camp Banner

9/2017 | Inspired by a graphic made by my sister, I wanted to create a winter feel but also incorporate (brown) graphic elements like from a campsite. The center mountain + rising sun symbolize leadership.

Winter Leadership Camp Logo

9/2017 | The event's logo, sort of a subset of the banner (previous).

Summer General Meeting #6 Banner

9/2017 | Silhouette of LA buildings with daytime at left and night time at right. I played around with gradients a lot this summer.

Summer General Meeting #3 Banner

7/2017 | Decided to trace the part of Bruin Plaza containing the famous Bruin Bear and make it a light color scheme.

Summer General Meeting #2 Banner

7/2017 | Intended to look like the night time vector version of Sunset Recreation Center at UCLA, a pool is at bottom with a grassy area next up (because sunset rec is known for the pools and field) and then trees and night sky in the back.

Ralph's + iGive Fundraiser

7/2017 | For a fundraiser that takes part of your spendings at Ralph's and donates it to our club. I clearly made it grocery shopping-themed and tried to include both logos for Ralph's and iGive without just throwing them in.

Trivia Tuesdays Template

6/2017 | A weekly social media series where members are asked questions about club history and structure. Design was inspired by an earlier social media graphic someone made; I used the same color scheme and general layout

GraphTech Committee Banner

5/2017 | The banner for the Graphic Design and Technology Committee's Facebook group. The rest of the graphics involving the committee had the same space theme/graphic elements. The awkward vertical spacing is to accommodate Facebook's web and mobile dimensions for group cover photos.

Bruin Day Flyer

4/2017 | The flyer we used to advertise Circle K at Bruin Day, an event where prospective new students visit UCLA to decide whether they want to attend or just to get a feel for the campus.

Assassins Graphic

2/2017 | One of the first cool graphics I made. This was my first attempts at tracing with Illustrator; I traced our club mascot, TurKey (turtle and a monkey), and then changed the colors to make him look evil (because the game was Assassins).

4/2016. This is the flyer I designed to be distributed to prospective UCLA students in hopes that they might join Circle K once enrolled.

Member of the Week Certificate

3/2017. The certificate awarded to the member of the week for demonstrating the club's 3 tenets: service, leadership, and fellowship.

Assassins Graphic

2/2017. The banner for assassins, a multi-week game within Circle K that ~150 members play until only one is left alive. It was created from scratch with vector elements by tracing over a picture of Turkey (turtle + monkey), our club mascot, in Adobe Illustrator.

Winter Leadership Retreat Overlay

12/2016. UCLA Circle K planned a large-scale leadership conference called Winter Leadership Camp. Another member created the primary event banner used to advertise it. Using similar elements and fonts from that original banner, I created this profile picture overlay that would be placed on top of members' Facebook profile pictures to further advertise the event.
