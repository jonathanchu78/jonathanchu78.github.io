ABOUT

Hello! My name is Jonathan, but you already knew that. I'm a Computer Science major and just finished my first year at UCLA.

Although my life as a programmer has just begun at UCLA, computers and coding have quickly taken root and grown as a substantial part of me. I am interested in web development and have dabbled in the field at hackathons, a startup, and with BuildUCLA at the UCLA Library, but in addition, I also love to explore how design, experience, and aesthetic contribute to the human experience.

When I'm not working on projects, you can often find me designing graphics or volunteering withUCLA Circle K.You can check out some of the graphics I've worked onhere.

While the primary reason for attending a 4-year university is to get a good education/degree for a future job and income, I strongly believe that not every moment of any person's life should be dedicated to studying or working. It is in moments of pleasure, friendship, and helping others that we gain a sense of who we are and why we're here.

As such, I constantly look for opportunities to better myself in ways both within and beyond Computer Science and have done so with organizations like Circle K,The Daily Bruinas part of the graphics team, andMorning Sign Outas an editor.

I also find opportunities during what free time I do have to develop my skill as a musician and hope to continue improvingas a pianistfor the rest of my life.
